# VOLUME I — PHONETICS & FOUNDATIONS

## 1. PURPOSE

This volume defines not only the sound system of Auren, but also introduces its basic structural logic for interpretation.

Auren is a constructed, evolving language where meaning is built through structured sound + context.

---

## 2. WHAT IS AUREN?

Auren is a symbolic language system designed to:
- encode meaning
- evolve through use
- support abstract and philosophical expression
- allow human and artificial interpretation

It is not static and does not have a final form.

---

## 3. WRITING SYSTEM (ALPHABET)

A E I O U  
B C D F G H J K L M N P R S T V X Z  

---

## 4. PHONETIC RULES

### 4.1 Pronunciation Units
Each letter is pronounced consistently.

Example:
- A = "a"
- E = "e"
- I = "i"

---

### 4.2 Digraphs (Combined Sounds)

TH = soft /θ/ or /t/ variation  
SH = "sh" sound  
KH = harsh /k/ breath sound  
ZH = voiced "zh" sound  

---

### 4.3 Syllable Structure

Allowed pattern:

(C)V(C)

Examples:
- nor
- vesh
- thal
- kron
- lum

---

### 4.4 Stress Rule

Stress falls on the penultimate syllable:

NO-ra  
VE-shar  
THA-len  
LU-men  

---

## 5. BASIC MEANING SYSTEM (INTRODUCTION)

Although meaning is defined in Volume III, Auren uses **semantic roots**:

### Core semantic anchors:

- nora = language / system of meaning
- vesh = idea / thought
- thal = knowledge / structured truth
- lumen = clarity / truth
- nox = absence / falsehood

These are foundational interpretive units.

---

## 6. FORMATION PRINCIPLE

Words in Auren can be:

- root-based (simple meaning)
- combined (compound meaning)
- evolved (modified over time)

Example:
vesh + thal → veshal (structured idea)

---

## 7. COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLE

Meaning in Auren is derived from:

1. structure
2. context
3. repetition
4. evolution of use

No word has a fully fixed meaning permanently.

---

## 8. EVOLUTION RULE

Auren is allowed to evolve:

- phonetics may expand
- meanings may shift
- new roots may appear
- obsolete forms may disappear

---

## 9. CORE PHILOSOPHICAL BASE

> Language is not a container of meaning.
> Language is a generator of meaning.

---

## 10. STATUS

This volume is foundational but not complete.
It is designed to support expansion in later volumes.
